Publications

Spellcasters VR

July 2017 - Aug 2017

https://team-spellcasters.itch.io/spellcasters-vr

Developed under the supervision of Against Gravity, creators of social VR game Rec Room.

Built with the goal of creating a truly social online VR experience, Spellcasters VR takes the fantasy of the magic duels from Harry Potter, and turns it into a team based competitive experience. Each player chooses their role (attacker, tank, healer), and draws gestures with their wands to summon offensive or defensive spells. Strategizing in the heat of battle is a must, as each role has unique abilities that will greatly benefit their team members. This project supports multiplatform players using both the HTC Vive and Oculus Rift to connect and play together.

I worked on implementing the round management and the multiplayer networking.

Tornado Tower

Jan 2017 - June 2017

https://tornado-tower.itch.io/tornado-tower

Tornado Tower is a VR third person platformer made in Unreal Engine 4 for the Oculus Touch. Watch the adventure of a young prince as he explores the Tornado Tower with the aid of a whimisical wind spirit to look for the person responsible for the death of his family.

I worked on implementing the camera panning, circular spline-based movement, wind and enemy behaviors, cinematics, and UI.

Pebble Lake VR

Jan 2017

https://globalgamejam.org/2017/games/pebble-lake

Pebble Lake is a relaxing VR zen rock skipping simulator made in Unity 5 for the HTC Vive. The game was created within 72 hours for the Global Game Jam 2017.

I worked on implementing the ripple effects on the water.

Fun Projects

Retain and Recollect

Oct 2016

https://globalgamejam.org/2017/games/pebble-lake

Solo student project based on two verbs: retain and recollect. This is a memory game where the player has 5 mins to familiarize with the locations of varying shapes with varying colors. After the duration is up, the player is assigned 5 colored shapes to search for.

Shouldn't Eaten

Oct 2016

https://tornado-tower.itch.io/tornado-tower

This is a 2-week narrative project based on the twist prompt "he shouldn't have eaten that". The board game allows up to 2-3 players where each player performs 2 actions per turn to explore the mysterious dungeon where rumors speak of a legendary fruit that resides within.

These are the adventurers that brave the dungeons in their quest to find the legendary fruit. Each start with their respective weapon and varying stats, might and speed, which determine how many dice the player rolls when performing certain actions. Yellow tokens represent items found in the dungeon whereas red tokens represent damage taken.

This is the first room where the players must solve the sphinx's riddle while constantly swarmed by vicious spiders.

This is the second room where the players must find the key to the exit while bandit mice hinder your progress by stealing your inventory.

This is the third room (in which I designed) where the players are trapped and must find the exits for the maze before the room fills up with water and they drown. The room is supposed to be revealed piece by piece as the players enter those respective regions.

This is the final room with the boss, and the twist! Will the adventurers prevail or perish?
